Scholarship Rio Tinto USA Family Scholarship Program
Application Deadline — No Exceptions
May 31, 2026
Apply Online Now →

Rio Tinto's Family Scholarship Program (FSP) provides $3,000 scholarships to child dependents of active U.S.-based Rio Tinto employees to help fund post-high school education. Full-time and part-time employees are eligible. Contractors, fixed-term, casual, and temporary employees are not eligible.

Who is Eligible
  • Child dependent of an active, eligible U.S.-based Rio Tinto employee (full-time or part-time)
  • Tax dependent for the prior federal tax year
  • Accepted at or attending an accredited post-secondary institution (university, college, trade school, etc.) in 2026
  • Minimum 3.0 cumulative GPA
  • Previous recipients may reapply — prior awards don't affect selection
  • Contractors, fixed-term, casual, and temporary employees are not eligible
  • Employees themselves do not qualify — this is for child dependents only
Required Documents
  • School transcripts (most recent; if unavailable before deadline, a screenshot from your school portal is accepted)
  • Personal essay
  • Letter of Recommendation — must be signed and include recommender contact info
  • Proof of enrollment — acceptance letter or copy of registered fall/summer courses
Payment Details
The $3,000 award is paid to the employee (not the child) through a regular payroll cycle. It is subject to Social Security, Medicare, and federal tax withholding and treated as ordinary income. The employee is responsible for passing the award to the child dependent — failure to do so may result in disqualification from the program.
Frequently Asked Questions
Can a student apply more than once?▾
Yes. Scholarships can be awarded year after year as long as eligibility requirements are met. Previous winners must reapply and go through the full selection process — last year's results have no bearing on this year's.
What schools qualify?▾
Any accredited post-secondary institution — universities, colleges, trade/technical schools. Graduate programs (masters, PhDs, certifications) are also eligible as this program covers all post-high school education.
What semester do I need to be enrolled in?▾
Either the coming summer or fall semester of 2026.
What if my grades aren't posted before the deadline?▾
A screenshot of your current grades from your school's website is accepted if official transcripts aren't available in time. Alternatively, your school can email the transcript directly to usafamilyscholarship@riotinto.com before the deadline.
What if I took a gap year?▾
Gap years for personal or religious reasons are welcome. Your cumulative GPA up to the point of the gap year will be considered.
Letter of Recommendation — what do I need to know?▾
The LOR must be signed and include the recommender's contact information. It doesn't have to be written specifically for this program, but a generic letter significantly reduces your chances. You can upload it yourself or have your recommender email it directly to usafamilyscholarship@riotinto.com before the deadline.
How are winners selected?▾
A committee of Rio Tinto employees from various business units reviews and scores all qualifying applications. Identifying information is removed prior to review. Each business unit has a pledged number of scholarships — students with the highest scores in each unit are selected. All decisions are final.
Do you accept mailed applications?▾
No. All applications must be submitted online at the link above.
